Background

During our community visits in Hubli, we encountered various social issues affecting the local population. A significant problem is the lack of support for individuals aspiring to start their own small home-based businesses. Many face obstacles due to limited access to essential services and a shortage of skilled labor, leading to financial difficulties.

Identified Issues

  • Women's Welfare Awareness

    There is a widespread belief that a woman is solely responsible for determining the gender of a child, particularly when a girl is born. This outdated thinking places unfair blame on women, reflecting a need for better education on gender and biology.

  • Unemployment

    Unemployment is a pressing issue, with some individuals remaining jobless for many years due to insufficient skills and training. Even well-educated individuals may struggle to find employment due to personal or logistical challenges, preventing them from reaching their full potential.

  • Infrastructure Problems

    Issues such as unsafe and improper electrical wiring installed by local authorities pose safety risks and highlight a need for greater accountability and quality control.

  • Financial Illiteracy

    Financial illiteracy remains a barrier for many people living in slum areas. Without adequate knowledge of basic financial principles, individuals are often lured into fraudulent schemes by illegal companies, trapping them in cycles of debt and exploitation.

Our Solution

To address these challenges, we propose connecting school dropouts and unemployed youth with the GMR Varalakshmi Foundation (GMRVF). This institution focuses on empowering individuals by providing them with the skills necessary for permanent employment. Many students come from slum areas and face significant financial challenges. The foundation offers a variety of training programs, including those under government schemes like the Pratibha scheme, aiming to equip these individuals with market-relevant skills.

Courses Offered

GMRVF offers a range of courses tailored for school dropouts and unemployed youth:

  • Basic Computer Training
  • Electrician Certification
  • Plumbing and Maintenance
  • Tailoring and Fashion Design
  • Entrepreneurship Development
  • Financial Literacy Programs
